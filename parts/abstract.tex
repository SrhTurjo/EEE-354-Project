\begin{abstract}
A non‑contact digital range finder was developed using an ESP32 microcontroller and an ultrasonic transducer. The instrument generates short bursts of ultrasound, measures the echo time of flight (ToF) and converts this to distance. A real‑time distance (10–200 cm) is displayed on an I²C liquid crystal display (LCD). Because the speed of sound depends on temperature and, to a lesser extent, humidity, this dependence is discussed in the measurement model. Signal processing techniques such as moving‑average filtering and Kalman filtering were explored to reduce measurement noise and improve accuracy. Calibration was carried out by comparing the sensor’s output with a standard measuring tape at various distances, and performance metrics including linearity and error percentage were computed.
\end{abstract}
